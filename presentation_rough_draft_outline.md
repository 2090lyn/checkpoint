# Rough Draft Outline - Checkpoint

## Project Form
Game-based presentation

- Format: playable game + spoken commentary
- Medium: interactive game systems used as rhetorical argument

## Claim
Life is often framed as one correct linear timeline, but lived experience is nonlinear and socially structured. This game uses pressure systems, repetition, and branching outcomes to challenge the myth of “falling behind.”

## What the Game Is Arguing
- Timeline anxiety is produced by systems, not personal failure.
- Productivity systems define worth through output and speed.
- Alternate paths always exist, even when the interface feels rigid.

## Why This Matters
- Students are constantly measured by deadlines and milestones.
- The game mirrors familiar pressure: optimize, keep up, avoid deviation.
- The ending reframes detours as possibility, not failure.

## Rough Outline of Game Content

### 1) Intro / Setup
- Player enters their name
- Game frames life as structured progression
- Objectives panel establishes external pressure immediately

### 2) High School Stage
- Core task themes: college applications, resume framing, major pressure
- Limited acceptable choices communicate social narrowing
- Socially discouraged choices trigger psychological strain effects

### 3) College Stage
- Task theme: internship pipeline and professionalization
- Includes coding/labor simulation to represent technical productivity expectations
- Reading assignment task appears as required checkpoint

### 4) Career Stage
- Looping options and repetitive labor mechanics intensify pressure
- Objectives mimic workplace systems (performance, inbox, metrics)
- Time pressure and repetition produce emotional fatigue

### 5) Marriage / Family / Retirement Stages
- Milestone progression mirrors social expectations beyond work
- Obligations stack; personal agency narrows
- Memory/reflection tasks expose accumulated compromise

### 6) Breakdown + Nonlinear Reveal
- Interface destabilizes as mental strain escalates
- Final reveal visualizes branching possibilities and unrealized paths
- Ending message rejects the single-track timeline model

## Mechanics-to-Meaning Map
- Timers and objective checklists: create urgency and “behind” feeling
- Forced/limited choices: model social constraint and norm enforcement
- Repetition loops: represent routine and structural inertia
- Strain effects (glitch, intrusive language): external pressure becomes internalized
- Branching end-state: demonstrates nonlinear temporality and contingency
